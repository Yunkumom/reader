# 9. Wealth Is What You Don’t See
## 9. 財富是你看不見的東西

### Paragraph 1

**[EN]** Money has many ironies. Here’s an important one: Wealth is what you don’t see.

**[ZH]** 金錢有許多諷刺之處。以下是很重要的一點：財富是你看不見的東西。

---

### Paragraph 2

**[EN]** My time as a valet was in the mid-2000s in Los Angeles, when material appearance took precedence over everything but oxygen.

**[ZH]** 我在 2000 年代中期於洛杉磯擔任泊車員；那是一個除了氧氣之外，物質外表凌駕一切的年代。

---

### Paragraph 3

**[EN]** If you see a Ferrari driving around, you might intuitively assume the owner of the car is rich—even if you’re not paying much attention to them.

**[ZH]** 如果你看到一輛法拉利在路上行駛，即使沒特別注意車主，也可能直覺認定他很有錢。

---

### Paragraph 4

**[EN]** But as I got to know some of these people I realized that wasn’t always the case.

**[ZH]** 但在認識其中一些人之後，我發現事實並非總是如此。

---

### Paragraph 5

**[EN]** Many were mediocre successes who spent a huge percentage of their paycheck on a car.

**[ZH]** 許多人只算取得普通程度的成功，卻把薪水極高的比例花在一輛車上。

---

### Paragraph 6

**[EN]** I remember a fellow we’ll call Roger. He was about my age.

**[ZH]** 我記得有個人，我們就叫他羅傑（Roger）。他和我年紀相仿。

---

### Paragraph 7

**[EN]** I had no idea what Roger did. But he drove a Porsche, which was enough for people to draw assumptions.

**[ZH]** 我完全不知道羅傑從事什麼工作，但他開著一輛保時捷（Porsche），這已足以讓人做出各種推測。

---

### Paragraph 8

**[EN]** Then one day Roger arrived in an old Honda. Same the next week, and the next.

**[ZH]** 後來有一天，羅傑開著一輛老舊本田（Honda）出現。下星期如此，再下星期也一樣。

---

### Paragraph 9

**[EN]** “What happened to your Porsche?” I asked. It was repossessed after defaulting on his car loan, he said.

**[ZH]** 「你的保時捷怎麼了？」我問。他說，因為汽車貸款違約，車被收回去了。

---

### Paragraph 10

**[EN]** There was not a morsel of shame. He responded like he was telling the next play in the game.

**[ZH]** 他毫無一絲羞愧，回答的口氣就像只是在描述比賽的下一個戰術。

---

### Paragraph 11

**[EN]** Every assumption you might have had about him was wrong. Los Angeles is full of Rogers.

**[ZH]** 你過去對他的所有推測全都錯了。洛杉磯到處都是羅傑。

---

### Paragraph 12

**[EN]** Someone driving a $100,000 car might be wealthy. But the only data point you have about their wealth is that they have $100,000 less than they did before they bought the car (or $100,000 more in debt). That’s all you know about them.

**[ZH]** 開著十萬美元汽車的人可能很富有，但你唯一掌握的財富資料是：買車之後，他比以前少了十萬美元（或者多了十萬美元債務）。關於他，你只知道這些。

---

### Paragraph 13

**[EN]** We tend to judge wealth by what we see, because that’s the information we have in front of us. We can’t see people’s bank accounts or brokerage statements. So we rely on outward appearances to gauge financial success.

**[ZH]** 我們傾向用看得見的事物判斷財富，因為那是眼前擁有的資訊。

---

### Paragraph 14

**[EN]** Cars. Homes. Instagram photos.

**[ZH]** 我們看不到別人的銀行帳戶或券商對帳單，因此只能依賴外在表象衡量財務成功：汽車、房屋、Instagram 照片。

---

### Paragraph 15

**[EN]** Modern capitalism makes helping people fake it until they make it a cherished industry.

**[ZH]** 現代資本主義把幫助人們「假裝成功，直到真正成功」發展成備受重視的產業。

---

### Paragraph 16

**[EN]** But the truth is that wealth is what you don’t see.

**[ZH]** 但真相是，財富是你看不見的東西。

---

### Paragraph 17

**[EN]** Wealth is the nice cars not purchased. The diamonds not bought. The watches not worn, the clothes forgone and the first-class upgrade declined.

**[ZH]** 財富是沒有買下的名車、沒有購買的鑽石、沒有戴上的手錶、放棄添購的衣服，以及婉拒的頭等艙升等。

---

### Paragraph 18

**[EN]** Wealth is financial assets that haven’t yet been converted into the stuff you see.

**[ZH]** 財富是尚未轉換成眼前物品的金融資產。

---

### Paragraph 19

**[EN]** That’s not how we think about wealth, because you can’t contextualize what you can’t see.

**[ZH]** 我們並不習慣這樣思考財富，因為無法看見的東西，也無法放進具體脈絡裡理解。

---

### Paragraph 20

**[EN]** Singer Rihanna nearly went bankrupt after overspending and sued her financial advisor. The advisor responded:

**[ZH]** 歌手蕾哈娜（Rihanna）曾因過度消費而差點破產，並控告她的財務顧問。顧問回應：

---

### Paragraph 21

**[EN]** “Was it really necessary to tell her that if you spend money on things, you will end up with the things and not the money?”³⁰

**[ZH]** 「真的有必要告訴她，如果把錢花在東西上，最後手上留下的是東西，而不是錢嗎？」³⁰

---

### Paragraph 22

**[EN]** You can laugh, and please do. But the answer is, yes, people do need to be told that.

**[ZH]** 你可以笑，而且請儘管笑。但答案是：對，人們的確需要有人提醒這件事。

---

### Paragraph 23

**[EN]** When most people say they want to be a millionaire, what they might actually mean is “I’d like to spend a million dollars.”

**[ZH]** 多數人說想成為百萬富翁時，真正的意思可能是：「我想花掉一百萬美元。」

---

### Paragraph 24

**[EN]** And that is literally the opposite of being a millionaire.

**[ZH]** 而這在字面上恰恰是成為百萬富翁的反面。

---

### Paragraph 25

**[EN]** Investor Bill Mann once wrote: “There is no faster way to feel rich than to spend lots of money on really nice things.

**[ZH]** 投資人比爾・曼（Bill Mann）曾寫道：「沒有任何方法能比花大把鈔票買真正高級的東西，更快讓你覺得自己富有。

---

### Paragraph 26

**[EN]** But the way to be rich is to spend money you have, and to not spend money you don’t have.

**[ZH]** 但真正變富的方法，是只花自己擁有的錢，不花自己沒有的錢。

---

### Paragraph 27

**[EN]** It’s really that simple.”³¹

**[ZH]** 事情真的就這麼簡單。」³¹

---

### Paragraph 28

**[EN]** It is excellent advice, but it may not go far enough.

**[ZH]** 這是絕佳建議，但可能還不夠徹底。

---

### Paragraph 29

**[EN]** The only way to be wealthy is to not spend the money that you do have.

**[ZH]** 變得富有的唯一方法，是不要花掉你已經擁有的錢。

---

### Paragraph 30

**[EN]** It’s not just the only way to accumulate wealth; it’s the very definition of wealth.

**[ZH]** 這不只是累積財富的唯一方法，更是財富本身的定義。

---

### Paragraph 31

**[EN]** We should be careful to define the difference between wealthy and rich. It is more than semantics.

**[ZH]** 我們必須謹慎區分「高收入」（rich）和「有財富」（wealthy）。這不只是語意差異。

---

### Paragraph 32

**[EN]** Not knowing the difference is a source of countless poor money decisions.

**[ZH]** 不了解兩者的區別，是無數糟糕金錢決策的來源。

---

### Paragraph 33

**[EN]** Rich is a current income. Someone driving a $100,000 car is almost certainly rich, because even if they purchased the car with debt you need a certain level of income to afford the monthly payment.

**[ZH]** 「高收入」指的是當前所得。開著十萬美元汽車的人幾乎肯定收入很高，因為即使是借錢買車，也需要一定程度的收入才能負擔每月車貸。

---

### Paragraph 34

**[EN]** Same with those who live in big homes.

**[ZH]** 住豪宅的人也是如此。

---

### Paragraph 35

**[EN]** It’s not hard to spot rich people. They often go out of their way to make themselves known.

**[ZH]** 高收入者不難辨認，他們往往還會特意讓自己受到注意。

---

### Paragraph 36

**[EN]** But wealth is hidden. It’s income not spent. Wealth is an option not yet taken to buy something later.

**[ZH]** 但財富是隱藏的，是沒有花掉的收入；是保留到未來購買某項東西、尚未行使的選擇權。

---

### Paragraph 37

**[EN]** Its value lies in offering you options, flexibility, and growth to one day purchase more stuff than you could right now.

**[ZH]** 它的價值，在於提供選擇、彈性與成長，使你有一天能買下比現在更多的東西。

---

### Paragraph 38

**[EN]** Diet and exercise offer a useful analogy.

**[ZH]** 飲食與運動提供了一個實用的類比。

---

### Paragraph 39

**[EN]** Losing weight is notoriously hard, even among those putting in the work of vigorous exercise.

**[ZH]** 減重出了名地困難，即使對努力進行高強度運動的人也一樣。

---

### Paragraph 40

**[EN]** In his book The Body, Bill Bryson explains why:

**[ZH]** 比爾・布萊森（Bill Bryson）在《身體》（The Body）一書中解釋原因：

---

### Paragraph 41

**[EN]** One study in America found that people overestimate the number of calories they burned in a workout by a factor of four. They also then consumed, on average, about twice as many calories as they had just burned off … the fact is, you can quickly undo a lot of exercise by eating a lot of food, and most of us do.

**[ZH]** 美國有項研究發現，人們高估自己運動所消耗的熱量達四倍之多；接著，他們平均又吃進剛消耗熱量的兩倍……事實是，只要吃下大量食物，就能迅速抵銷大量運動，而我們大多數人正是如此。

---

### Paragraph 42

**[EN]** Exercise is like being rich. You think, “I did the work and I now deserve to treat myself to a big meal.”

**[ZH]** 運動就像高收入。你會想：「我努力過了，現在應該用一頓大餐犒賞自己。」

---

### Paragraph 43

**[EN]** Wealth is turning down that treat meal and actually burning net calories. It’s hard, and requires self-control. But it creates a gap between what you could do and what you choose to do that accrues to you over time.

**[ZH]** 財富則是拒絕那頓大餐，真正消耗淨熱量。這很困難，需要自制力，卻能在「你有能力做的事」與「你選擇去做的事」之間創造差距，而這份差距會隨時間累積到你身上。

---

### Paragraph 44

**[EN]** The problem for many of us is that it is easy to find rich role models. It’s harder to find wealthy ones because by definition their success is more hidden.

**[ZH]** 許多人的問題是，高收入的榜樣很好找，真正富有的榜樣卻難得多，因為依照定義，他們的成功更為隱蔽。

---

### Paragraph 45

**[EN]** There are, of course, wealthy people who also spend a lot of money on stuff.

**[ZH]** 當然，也有真正富有、同時花很多錢買東西的人。

---

### Paragraph 46

**[EN]** But even in those cases what we see is their richness, not their wealth. We see the cars they chose to buy and perhaps the school they choose to send their kids to. We don’t see the savings, retirement accounts, or investment portfolios.

**[ZH]** 但即使在那些案例中，我們看到的仍是他們的高收入，而不是財富。我們看見他們選擇購買的汽車，或送孩子就讀的學校，卻看不到儲蓄、退休帳戶或投資組合。

---

### Paragraph 47

**[EN]** We see the homes they bought, not the homes they could have bought had they stretched themselves thin.

**[ZH]** 我們看見他們買下的房子，卻看不見如果把財務拉到極限，他們原本有能力買下的房子。

---

### Paragraph 48

**[EN]** The danger here is that I think most people, deep down, want to be wealthy.

**[ZH]** 這裡的危險在於，我認為多數人內心真正想要的是財富。

---

### Paragraph 49

**[EN]** They want freedom and flexibility, which is what financial assets not yet spent can give you.

**[ZH]** 他們想要自由與彈性，而尚未花用的金融資產正能提供這些。

---

### Paragraph 50

**[EN]** But it is so ingrained in us that to have money is to spend money that we don’t get to see the restraint it takes to actually be wealthy.

**[ZH]** 但「有錢就是把錢花掉」的觀念在我們心中根深柢固，以致我們看不見真正變富所需要的克制。

---

### Paragraph 51

**[EN]** And since we can’t see it, it’s hard to learn about it.

**[ZH]** 既然看不見，也就難以學習。

---

### Paragraph 52

**[EN]** People are good at learning by imitation. But the hidden nature of wealth makes it hard to imitate others and learn from their ways.

**[ZH]** 人類擅長透過模仿學習，但財富的隱蔽本質，使我們很難模仿他人、從他們的方法中學習。

---

### Paragraph 53

**[EN]** After he died, Ronald Read became many people’s financial role model. He was lionized in the media and cherished on social media.

**[ZH]** 羅納德・里德（Ronald Read）過世後，成為許多人的理財榜樣；媒體盛讚他，社群媒體也推崇他。

---

### Paragraph 54

**[EN]** But he was nobody’s financial role model while he was living because every penny of his wealth was hidden, even to those who knew him.

**[ZH]** 但他活著時從未成為任何人的理財榜樣，因為每一分財富都隱藏起來，甚至連認識他的人也看不見。

---

### Paragraph 55

**[EN]** Imagine how hard it would be to learn how to write if you couldn’t read the works of great authors. Who would be your inspiration?

**[ZH]** 想像一下，如果你看不到偉大作家的作品，要學會寫作會有多困難。誰會啟發你？

---

### Paragraph 56

**[EN]** Who would you admire? Whose nuanced tricks and tips would you follow?

**[ZH]** 你會仰慕誰？又會追隨誰細膩的技巧與訣竅？

---

### Paragraph 57

**[EN]** It would make something that is already hard even harder.

**[ZH]** 這會讓原本就很困難的事變得更加困難。

---

### Paragraph 58

**[EN]** It’s difficult to learn from what you can’t see. Which helps explain why it’s so hard for many to build wealth.

**[ZH]** 人很難從看不見的東西學習，也因此可以解釋，為何許多人如此難以累積財富。

---

### Paragraph 59

**[EN]** The world is filled with people who look modest but are actually wealthy and people who look rich who live at the razor’s edge of insolvency.

**[ZH]** 這個世界到處都是外表樸素、實際上非常富有的人，也到處都是看似有錢、其實生活在破產邊緣的人。

---

### Paragraph 60

**[EN]** Keep this in mind when quickly judging others’ success and setting your own goals.

**[ZH]** 在匆忙評判他人的成功與設定自身目標時，請記住這一點。

---

**[EN]** If wealth is what you don’t spend, what good is it? Well, let me convince you to save money.

**[ZH]** 如果財富是你沒有花掉的錢，那它究竟有什麼用？好，接下來讓我說服你儲蓄。
