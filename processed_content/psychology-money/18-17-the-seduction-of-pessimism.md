### Paragraph 3

**[ZH]** 然而，悲觀主義在我們心中占有特殊地位。悲觀不只比樂觀更普遍，聽起來也更聰明。

### Paragraph 5

**[EN]** It’s intellectually captivating, and it’s paid more attention than optimism, which is often viewed as being oblivious to risk.

**[ZH]** 它在知性上令人著迷，獲得的關注也多於樂觀；樂觀往往被視為對風險毫無察覺。

### Paragraph 6

**[ZH]** 繼續之前，我們應先定義什麼是樂觀。

### Paragraph 7

**[ZH]** 真正的樂觀主義者並不認為一切都會很美好，那叫自滿。

---

### Paragraph 8

**[EN]** Optimism is a belief that the odds of a good outcome are in your favor over time, even when there will be setbacks along the way.

**[ZH]** 樂觀，是相信儘管一路會遭遇挫折，但隨著時間推移，好結果的機率終究站在你這邊。

---

### Paragraph 9

**[EN]** The simple idea that most people wake up in the morning trying to make things a little better and more productive than wake up looking to cause trouble is the foundation of optimism. It’s not complicated. It’s not guaranteed, either.

**[ZH]** 多數人早晨醒來，是想讓事情變得好一點、更有生產力，而不是想製造麻煩；這個簡單觀念就是樂觀的基礎。它並不複雜，當然也沒有保證。只是對多數人而言，在多數時候，它是最合理的押注。

---

### Paragraph 10

**[EN]** It’s just the most reasonable bet for most people, most of the time. The late statistician Hans Rosling put it differently: “I am not an optimist. I am a very serious possibilist.”

**[ZH]** 已故統計學家漢斯・羅斯林（Hans Rosling）換了一種說法：「我不是樂觀主義者，我是一個極其認真的可能性主義者。」
