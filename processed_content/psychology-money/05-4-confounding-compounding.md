### Paragraph 20

**[EN]** In the early 1900s a Serbian scientist named Milutin Milanković studied the Earth’s position relative to other planets and came up with the theory of ice ages that we now know is accurate: The gravitational pull of the sun and moon gently affect the Earth’s motion and tilt toward the sun. During parts of this cycle—which can last tens of thousands of years—each of the Earth’s hemispheres gets a little more, or a little less, solar radiation than they’re used to.

**[ZH]** 二十世紀初，塞爾維亞科學家米盧廷・米蘭科維奇（Milutin Milanković）研究地球相對於其他行星的位置，提出如今已知正確的冰河時期理論：太陽與月球的重力，會輕微影響地球運動，以及地軸相對太陽的傾角。在這個可能持續數萬年的循環中，地球兩半球有時會比平常多接收一點太陽輻射，有時則少一點。
