# 8. Man in the Car Paradox
## 8. 車中人的弔詭

### Paragraph 1

**[EN]** The best part of being a valet is getting to drive some of the coolest cars to ever touch pavement.

**[ZH]** 當泊車員最棒的部分，就是能駕駛一些有史以來最酷、真正上過路面的汽車。

---

### Paragraph 2

**[EN]** Guests came in driving Ferraris, Lamborghinis, Rolls- Royces—the whole aristocratic fleet.

**[ZH]** 客人開著法拉利（Ferrari）、藍寶堅尼（Lamborghini）、勞斯萊斯（Rolls-Royce）前來——整支貴族車隊一應俱全。

---

### Paragraph 3

**[EN]** It was my dream to have one of these cars of my own, because (I thought) they sent such a strong signal to others that you made it. You’re smart. You’re rich.

**[ZH]** 我夢想擁有其中一輛，因為我以為這些車能向別人發出強烈訊號，證明你已功成名就：你很聰明、很富有、有品味、很重要。

---

### Paragraph 4

**[EN]** You have taste. You’re important. Look at me.

**[ZH]** 看看我吧。

---

### Paragraph 5

**[EN]** The irony is that I rarely if ever looked at them, the drivers.

**[ZH]** 諷刺的是，我幾乎從來沒有看過駕駛人本人。

---

### Paragraph 6

**[EN]** When you see someone driving a nice car, you rarely think, “Wow, the guy driving that car is cool.”

**[ZH]** 當你看見有人駕駛一輛好車時，很少會想：「哇，開這輛車的人真酷。」

---

### Paragraph 7

**[EN]** Instead, you think, “Wow, if I had that car people would think I’m cool.”

**[ZH]** 你反而會想：「哇，如果我有這輛車，大家就會覺得我很酷。」

---

### Paragraph 8

**[EN]** Subconscious or not, this is how people think.

**[ZH]** 無論是否出於潛意識，人們就是這樣思考。

---

### Paragraph 9

**[EN]** There is a paradox here: people tend to want wealth to signal to others that they should be liked and admired.

**[ZH]** 其中存在一個弔詭：人們往往渴望財富，好向他人發出訊號，讓對方喜歡、仰慕自己。

---

### Paragraph 10

**[EN]** But in reality those other people often bypass admiring you, not because they don’t think wealth is admirable, but because they use your wealth as a benchmark for their own desire to be liked and admired.

**[ZH]** 但事實上，那些人通常根本略過了對你的仰慕。這不是因為他們不認為財富值得欽羨，而是因為他們把你的財富當作衡量基準，投射到自己想被喜愛與仰慕的欲望上。

---

### Paragraph 11

**[EN]** The letter I wrote after my son was born said, “You might think you want an expensive car, a fancy watch, and a huge house.

**[ZH]** 我在兒子出生後寫給他的信中說：「你可能以為自己想要昂貴的汽車、精美的手錶和巨大的房子。

---

### Paragraph 12

**[EN]** But I’m telling you, you don’t.

**[ZH]** 但我要告訴你，你並不是真的想要這些。

---

### Paragraph 13

**[EN]** What you want is respect and admiration from other people, and you think having expensive stuff will bring it.

**[ZH]** 你想要的是別人的尊重與欽佩，而你以為擁有昂貴物品就能帶來這些。

---

### Paragraph 14

**[EN]** It almost never does—especially from the people you want to respect and admire you.”

**[ZH]** 實際上幾乎從來不會——尤其無法從你真正希望尊重、欽佩你的人身上得到。」

---

### Paragraph 15

**[EN]** I learned that as a valet, when I began thinking about all the people driving up to the hotel in their Ferraris, watching me gawk.

**[ZH]** 我當泊車員時學會了這件事。當時我開始思考所有開著法拉利抵達飯店、看著我目瞪口呆的人。

---

### Paragraph 16

**[EN]** People must gawk everywhere they went, and I’m sure they loved it. I’m sure they felt admired.

**[ZH]** 他們走到哪裡，大概都有人盯著車看，而我相信他們也很享受。我相信他們覺得自己備受仰慕。

---

### Paragraph 17

**[EN]** But did they know I did not care about them, or even notice them?

**[ZH]** 但他們知道我根本不在乎他們，甚至沒注意到他們嗎？

---

### Paragraph 18

**[EN]** Did they know I was only gawking at the car, and imagining myself in the driver’s seat?

**[ZH]** 他們知道我只是在盯著車，幻想自己坐在駕駛座上嗎？

---

### Paragraph 19

**[EN]** Did they buy a Ferrari thinking it would bring them admiration without realizing that I—and likely most others—who are impressed with the car didn’t actually give them,

**[ZH]** 他們是否以為買一輛法拉利會為自己帶來仰慕，卻沒發現我——以及很可能大多數被車吸引的人——

---

### Paragraph 20

**[EN]** the driver, a moment’s thought?

**[ZH]** 其實連一秒都沒有想到駕駛人？

---

### Paragraph 21

**[EN]** Does this same idea apply to those living in big homes? Almost certainly.

**[ZH]** 這個道理同樣適用於住在豪宅裡的人嗎？幾乎可以肯定。

---

### Paragraph 22

**[EN]** Jewelry and clothes? Yep.

**[ZH]** 珠寶與衣服呢？也一樣。

---

### Paragraph 23

**[EN]** My point here is not to abandon the pursuit of wealth. Or even fancy cars. I like both.

**[ZH]** 我的重點不是要你放棄追求財富，甚至也不是要你放棄名車。我兩者都喜歡。

---

### Paragraph 24

**[EN]** It’s a subtle recognition that people generally aspire to be respected and admired by others,

**[ZH]** 我只是想提醒一個細微的事實：人們通常渴望獲得他人的尊重與欽佩，

---

### Paragraph 25

**[EN]** and using money to buy fancy things may bring less of it than you imagine.

**[ZH]** 但用金錢購買華麗物品，能帶來的尊重與欽佩可能比你想像中少。

---

### Paragraph 26

**[EN]** If respect and admiration are your goal, be careful how you seek it.

**[ZH]** 如果尊重與欽佩是你的目標，請謹慎選擇追求方式。

---

### Paragraph 27

**[EN]** Humility, kindness, and empathy will bring you more respect than horsepower ever will.

**[ZH]** 謙遜、善良與同理心能為你帶來的尊重，遠勝過再強大的馬力。

---

### Paragraph 28

**[EN]** We’re not done talking about Ferraris. Another story about the paradox of fast cars in the next chapter.

**[ZH]** 我們還沒談完法拉利。下一章還有另一個關於跑車弔詭的故事。
